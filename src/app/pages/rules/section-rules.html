TF2Stadium Rules

TL;DR: DON’T BE A JERK

Below you will find a concise list of rules designed to help explain what we allow on our site. Unfortunately, no set of rules could cover all future possibilities, so we expect you to use your common sense and follow the spirit of the rules.

If you have a question about one of these rules or have doubts about something you want to do, please post on our steam group or contact a staff member for clarification.

All rule violations are handled on a case-by-case basis. For information about how we handle site violations and how to appeal a punishment, please refer to the Punishments Page.

General Site Rules

  1. Do not engage in threatening behavior.

    Threats and malicious actions towards this site, other sites, and other members is unacceptable. We treat this rule very seriously and will punish violations harshly.

    Examples of threatening behavior include, but are not limited to, threats or attempts to DDoS, dox, hack, swat, or attack other members.

  2. Do not harass or flame other members.

    Harassing, flaming, and excessive trolling of other members is not allowed.

    This rule includes, but is not limited to, the use of offensive, racist, sexist, and other derogatory language.

  3. Do not cheat or abuse exploits.

    The use of third party tools and game or map exploits to gain an unfair advantage is not allowed.

    Examples include, but are not limited to, accessing the skybox of a map, using texture cheats to see through walls, or using an aim assistance tool.

  4. Do not abuse the site.

    Do not attempt to disrupt other members’ usage of the site.

    Examples include, but are not limited to, attempting attacks on the site or spamming

  5. Do not post, display, or distribute adult or vulgar content.

    This rule covers on-site chat, the forum, mumble, or on profiles.

    Examples include, but are not limited to, pornography, morbid content, and screamers.

  6. Do not attempt to avoid punishments.

    The use of alternate accounts, changing IPs, and other punishment avoidance techniques are not allowed.

    If you feel that you have been punished unfairly, please refer to the Punishments Page for more information on the appeal process.

Chat Etiquette

  1. Do not spam.

    On-site chat is not the place to paste your “copy-pasta” or post ascii images.

  2. Use English.

    Unfortunately, it would put an incredible burden on our staff to moderate chat and issues in languages other than English.

  3. Avoid these topics

    While we are strong proponents of free speech, unfortunately there are some topics we have to restrict to keep the experience great for all of our users. We will only add items if they become serious problems:

    1. Arguing with staff about site bans or rules. Please use the appropriate sections in the forums instead.
    2. Soliciting trades. This is not a trading site. Instead, please use one of the several trading sites available to the community.
    3. Encouraging other users to break a site rule.
  4. Do not encourage or discuss illegal activities.

    Please comply with US, UK, and EU laws.

Lobby Leader Etiquette

  1. Do not abuse on-site kick/ban tools.

    Our site is for the enjoyment of all of its members, and the usage of lobby kick/bans to harass a user is unacceptable. Banning someone from your lobby should be considered a serious action and should not be done without a reason.

    An example of a valid kick/ban would be to remove someone who trolls you repeatedly.

    An example of an invalid kick/ban would be to remove someone because you do not believe they are good enough. Instead use the lobby slot restriction tools available to you.

  2. Server admin abuse is not allowed.

    Abuse includes, but is not limited to, kicking a player from the server, enabling sv_cheats, or otherwise changing expected competitive gameplay.

  3. Make sure your server is adequate.

    Have the appropriate configurations and whitelists. Disable any gameplay changing server mods. Do not use a server hosted on your own computer.

    For more information on preferable server configurations, read the About Servers page.
